# RFK Jr. — "heavy metals in our food"

Illustrative debate-prep note. The point of this note is the **magnitude** move: a claim
can be *technically true* (trace heavy metals are detectable in some foods) while the
implied magnitude ("our food is poisoning kids") is unsupported. Keep the actual numbers
and the regulatory context one query away.

## The verifiable baseline
The FDA's *Closer to Zero* program sets action levels for lead, arsenic, cadmium and
mercury in foods commonly eaten by babies and young children, and reports that dietary
exposure for most children is low relative to those levels. Detectable ≠ harmful dose —
the dose and the action level are the whole argument.

- FDA, Closer to Zero action plan. https://www.fda.gov/food/environmental-contaminants-food/closer-zero-reducing-childhood-exposure-contaminants-foods
- FDA, Total Diet Study (ongoing dietary exposure monitoring). https://www.fda.gov/food/science-research-food/total-diet-study

## How to use it live
When the claim is "there are heavy metals in X," ask for the **action level** and the
**measured exposure**. The gap between "present" and "above the action level" is the
magnitude lie. Cite FDA Closer to Zero, not a headline.
