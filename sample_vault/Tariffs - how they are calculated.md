# Tariffs — who pays, and the "reciprocal" formula

Illustrative note. Two separate claims get conflated in tariff debates: (1) *who bears the
cost* and (2) *whether a given tariff rate is derived from anything coherent*.

## Who pays
The economics literature finds the cost of recent US tariffs was borne largely by domestic
importers and consumers, not fully by exporting countries — a magnitude claim you can source.

- Amiti, M., Redding, S. J., & Weinstein, D. E. (2019). The Impact of the 2018 Tariffs on Prices and Welfare. *Journal of Economic Perspectives*, 33(4), 187–210. https://www.aeaweb.org/articles?id=10.1257/jep.33.4.187
- Fajgelbaum, P. et al. (2020). The Return to Protectionism. *Quarterly Journal of Economics*, 135(1). https://academic.oup.com/qje/article/135/1/1/5626442

## Derivation of a "reciprocal" rate
If a headline tariff rate equals (trade deficit with a country ÷ imports from that country),
that is not a tariff on measured trade barriers — it's an accounting ratio. Being able to
pull the definition and the source instantly is the whole game.
