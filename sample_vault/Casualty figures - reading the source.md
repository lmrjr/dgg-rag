# Reading casualty figures — provenance beats the number

Illustrative methodology note (topic-neutral). In any conflict debate the number itself is
less important than **who counted, how, and what the number includes**. The magnitude lie
here is quoting a figure without its definition (combatants vs. civilians, time window,
verification method).

## Checklist to attach to any casualty number
1. Source body and its method (registry vs. estimate vs. survey model).
2. Inclusion criteria — what population and time window.
3. Whether it is a count or a modeled estimate (with a confidence interval).

- On why counting method drives the number: Human Rights Data Analysis Group, methods overview. https://hrdag.org/coreconcepts/
- Example of a modeled excess-mortality estimate vs. a direct count (general reference): The Lancet excess-mortality methodology discussions. https://www.thelancet.com/

## Statistician's note
A modeled estimate and a verified count are not the same object; comparing them as if equal
is a category error. This is precisely where a statistics review adds value to a debate desk.
